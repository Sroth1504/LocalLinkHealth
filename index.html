The challenge

No frontline worker can know everything

Every area has hundreds of local services across housing, mental health, social care, substance misuse, financial support, and community provision. No single worker can hold all of that in their head. People fall through the gaps — not because workers don't care, but because the system is too complex to navigate alone. As neighbourhood working and integrated care accelerate, the pressure on frontline staff to know more, join more dots, and do more with less has never been greater.

How it works

Three steps to the right referral

LocalLink works the way frontline professionals think — in plain English, about real people in real situations.

1

Describe the situation

Type a plain English description of the person's circumstances — no forms, no codes, no jargon. No names or identifying details needed.

2

AI matches services

LocalLink reads the situation and matches it against a verified directory of local Tameside services, ranking them by relevance to this specific person.

3

Get a referral summary

Receive a navigator's assessment, ranked service recommendations with contact details, and a printable referral summary — available in 10+ languages.

Who it's for

Built for everyone who works with people

LocalLink works for any public service professional who needs to connect people to the right support — whoever employs them, wherever they work.

NHS & Primary Care

GPs, nurses, receptionists, social prescribers, community nurses, and hospital discharge teams connecting people with community and social care support.

Local Government

Housing officers, social care staff, benefits advisers, customer service teams, and community safety workers helping residents access the right support.

VCSE & Community

Voluntary organisations, foodbanks, domestic abuse services, faith groups, and community hubs working alongside statutory services.

Schools & Education

School staff, inclusion leads, and early help teams identifying and referring pupils and families to the right community support.

Police & Justice

Officers, PCSOs, probation staff, and criminal justice workers connecting people with the support that reduces reoffending and vulnerability.

Neighbourhood Teams

Place-based teams, integrated neighbourhood hubs, and multi-agency partnerships working together under integrated care and neighbourhood health models.

Features

Everything a navigator needs

Plain English search

Describe a situation naturally — no codes, no categories. LocalLink understands context, not just keywords.

Printable referral summaries

One-click print summary with navigator assessment, top services, contact details, and worker notes box.

10+ language translation

Print referral summaries in Arabic, Urdu, Polish, Bengali, Punjabi, Somali, Chinese, French and more — with dialect options.

Live verified directory

Services are maintained in a live database — updates appear automatically within minutes, no code changes needed.

Privacy by design

No personal data entered. No names, dates of birth, or NHS numbers. Described in general terms only.

Usage analytics

Search logs, popular queries, and service match data — giving organisations evidence of impact and usage patterns.

The bigger picture

The right referral at the right time saves the system money

The NHS 10 Year Plan sets a clear direction — shift care closer to home, invest in prevention, build neighbourhood health teams that work across organisational boundaries. LocalLink is the practical tool that makes that vision real at the point of contact.

Earlier intervention

Getting the right referral first time prevents escalation to higher-cost acute services. A housing referral today avoids an A&E attendance next month.

Neighbourhood working

LocalLink supports the shift to place-based, multi-agency teams by giving every worker — regardless of employer — the same shared knowledge of local provision.

Reduced duplication

A consistent, verified local directory means every worker is working from the same information — reducing wasted contacts, wrong referrals, and frustrated citizens.

About

Built by someone who works in the system

LocalLink was founded by Sam Rothwell, an NHS Operations Manager with direct frontline experience of the challenges public service workers face navigating local provision. The NHS 10 Year Plan, neighbourhood health models, and integrated care frameworks all point in the same direction — earlier intervention, better coordination, and less duplication. LocalLink makes that happen at the moment of contact.

Every unnecessary A&E attendance, every missed social care referral, every family that falls between services represents both a human cost and a system cost. LocalLink reduces both — by giving every worker, regardless of their experience level, access to the right answer at the right moment.
